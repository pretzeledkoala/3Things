# Poincar√© Duality 
<theorem>

If $M$ is an $n$-dimensional oriented closed manifold (compact and without boundary), then the $k$ th cohomology group of $M$ is isomorphic to the $(n-k)$ th homology group of $M$:
$$
H^k(M)\cong H_{n-k}(M)
$$

</theorem>

# Quantum Cohomology 

<definition>

Two equivalent definitions of **quantum cohomology** $\text{QH}(X)$: 

1. the free $\mathbb{Z}[q]$-module with basis $\{[X^\lambda]\}$, or 
2. $H^*(X)\otimes_{\mathbb{Z}} \mathbb{Z}[q]$.

</definition>

# Gromov-Witten Invariant

<definition>

Given a projective variety $X$ and $|\lambda| + |\mu| + |\nu| = \dim(X) + d$, the **Gromov-Witten invariant** is
$$
    \langle X^\lambda, X^\mu, X^\nu \rangle = \# \left\{ \phi: \mathbb{P}^1 \to X \mid \deg(\phi) = d\right\}.
$$

</definition>
